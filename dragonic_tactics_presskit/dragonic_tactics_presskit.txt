DESCRIPTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Dragonic Tactics flips the classic fantasy dungeon crawl on its head: instead
of a party of heroes hunting a monster, you are the monster. You command a
powerful dragon defending its lair against a band of adventurers - a Fighter, a
Cleric, a Rogue, and a Wizard - who arrive to slay you. Hold the lair, and the
invaders fall; lose the dragon, and they win.

Combat is a turn-based, grid-based tactics duel built on tabletop foundations.
Every attack and defense is resolved with dice rolls, every move spends movement
and action points, and every spell is drawn from a limited pool of leveled spell
slots. The dragon is a one-versus-many powerhouse - high health, long reach, and
the deepest spellbook in the game - but the adventurers coordinate, heal, buff,
debuff, and flank, so raw power alone is not enough.

The dragon's toolkit rewards planning over button-mashing. Upcasting lets you
spend a higher-level slot to amplify a spell, so you constantly weigh burst power
against conserving resources. Terrain-altering spells let you reshape the
battlefield - raise walls to cut off attackers or spit lava to deny ground -
while status effects like Fear, Curse, Blessing, Haste, and Stealth swing the
math of every exchange.

Each of the four invaders is driven by its own hand-authored AI, designed as a
decision flowchart before it was ever coded: the Fighter presses the attack, the
Cleric heals and buffs from the back, the Rogue strikes from stealth, and the
Wizard kites from range. They read the board and react, so no two fights play out
the same way.

The game runs across three escalating encounters - a single duel, a two-enemy
skirmish, and a full four-adventurer raid - on a custom C++20 OpenGL engine that
ships to both Windows and the browser via WebAssembly.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HOW TO PLAY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Objective. You control the dragon. Defeat every adventurer that invades the map
to win the encounter. If the dragon is killed, the invaders win.

The turn. The dragon and the AI take alternating turns. On your turn you have
Movement (tiles you can travel), Action Points (AP, spent on attacks and spells),
and Spell Slots (by level). Movement and AP refill at the start of each turn, but
spell slots do not recover on their own - spend them deliberately.

Core loop. Position the dragon, pick targets, and resolve attacks via dice rolls;
reshape the board with walls and lava; apply status effects to tilt the odds;
then end your turn and watch the adventurers respond. Upcasting lets you sink a
higher-level slot into a spell to boost its effect when a fight demands it.

Controls.
  - Left-click  - select the dragon/tiles, move, and use skills (move by clicking
                  a highlighted tile; cast by clicking a spell icon then a target).
  - Right-click - cancel the current skill/range selection.
  - Slot bar (bottom of the screen) - basic attack, spell slots, and the End Turn
                  button.
  - Hover a character - inspect HP, AP, Movement, and Spell Slots; hover the
                  dragon to preview its movement range.
  - Esc - pause / return to menu.   F2 - screenshot.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FEATURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Play as the dragon. A role-reversal premise - you're the boss monster,
    defending your lair against the heroes.
  - Asymmetric one-vs-many tactics. A single powerful dragon against a coordinated
    party of up to four adventurers.
  - Tabletop-style combat. Dice-based attack/defense resolution, action points,
    movement, and leveled spell slots.
  - Deep dragon spellbook with upcasting. Spend higher-level slots to amplify
    spells - trade burst power against resource conservation.
  - Battlefield manipulation. Terrain-altering spells raise walls and spit lava to
    reshape the grid; knockback shoves enemies into hazards.
  - 9 status effects (Fear, Curse, Blessing, Haste, Stealth, Lifesteal, Frenzy,
    Exhaustion, Purify) and 20+ spells across the cast.
  - Four hand-authored AI archetypes. Fighter (aggressor), Cleric (support/healer),
    Rogue (stealth striker), and Wizard (ranged kiter) - each designed as a
    decision flowchart.
  - Three escalating encounters. A 5x7 duel, an 8x8 two-enemy skirmish, and a
    10x10 four-adventurer raid.
  - Custom engine, two platforms. Built on an in-house C++20 OpenGL engine; runs
    on Windows and in the browser via WebAssembly.
